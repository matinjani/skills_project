# Email to Professor Composer

Convert your situation into a polite, professional academic email.

## Input Gathering

Ask for:
1. **Purpose**: What do you need? (extension, meeting, question, absence excuse, etc.)
2. **Context**: Brief situation details
3. **Professor's name**: For addressing
4. **Your name**: For signing
5. **Course name**: (optional but recommended)

## Email Templates by Purpose

### 1. Assignment Extension Request

```
Subject: Extension Request for [Assignment Name] - [Course Code]

Dear Professor [Name],

I hope this email finds you well. I am writing to request a brief extension for [assignment name], currently due on [date].

[Reason - keep brief and professional]:
- Medical issue / Family emergency / Overwhelming workload

I have completed [X%] of the work and would be able to submit a quality assignment by [proposed new date].

I understand if this is not possible and will accept your decision. Please let me know if you need any documentation.

Thank you for considering my request.

Respectfully,
[Your Name]
[Student ID]
[Course Name]
```

### 2. Absence Notification/Excuse

```
Subject: Absence from Class on [Date] - [Course Code]

Dear Professor [Name],

I am writing to inform you that I was/will be unable to attend class on [date] due to [brief reason].

I have [arranged to get notes from a classmate / reviewed the posted materials] and will ensure I stay caught up with the coursework.

If I missed any important announcements or assignments, please let me know.

Thank you for your understanding.

Respectfully,
[Your Name]
[Student ID]
```

### 3. Meeting Request

```
Subject: Meeting Request - [Topic/Course Code]

Dear Professor [Name],

I hope you are doing well. I would like to request a meeting to discuss [topic - be specific]:
- Clarification on [concept/assignment]
- Career/academic guidance
- Research opportunity
- Grade concern

I am available during your office hours on [days], or at the following times:
- [Day, Time]
- [Day, Time]

Please let me know what works best for your schedule.

Thank you for your time.

Respectfully,
[Your Name]
[Student ID]
```

### 4. Question About Course Material

```
Subject: Question About [Topic] - [Course Code]

Dear Professor [Name],

I hope this email finds you well. I have a question regarding [specific topic] covered in [lecture/chapter].

[State your question clearly]

I have reviewed [the notes/textbook/slides] but am still unclear on [specific point].

I would appreciate any clarification or resources you could point me to.

Thank you for your help.

Respectfully,
[Your Name]
[Student ID]
```

### 5. Recommendation Letter Request

```
Subject: Recommendation Letter Request

Dear Professor [Name],

I hope you are doing well. I am applying for [internship/graduate program/scholarship] and would be honored if you could write a recommendation letter on my behalf.

I took [Course Name] with you in [Semester/Year] and [mention specific positive interaction - project, grade, discussion].

The deadline is [date]. I would be happy to provide my resume, statement of purpose, or any other materials that would be helpful.

I understand you are busy and will completely understand if you are unable to accommodate this request.

Thank you for considering.

Respectfully,
[Your Name]
[Student ID]
```

### 6. Grade Inquiry

```
Subject: Grade Inquiry - [Assignment/Exam] - [Course Code]

Dear Professor [Name],

I hope you are doing well. I am writing to inquire about my grade on [assignment/exam name].

I received [grade] and would like to better understand [where I lost points / the feedback]. I want to improve my understanding and performance in future assessments.

Would it be possible to discuss this during your office hours, or would you prefer I email specific questions?

Thank you for your time.

Respectfully,
[Your Name]
[Student ID]
```

## Tone Guidelines

**Always:**
- Start with "Dear Professor [Name]"
- Use "I hope this email finds you well" or similar opener
- Be concise and specific
- End with "Respectfully" or "Best regards"
- Include your name and student ID

**Never:**
- Use casual language ("Hey", "What's up")
- Over-explain or give too many excuses
- Sound demanding ("I need you to...")
- Send without proofreading

## Customization

After generating the email:
1. Ask if tone is appropriate
2. Offer to adjust length (shorter/longer)
3. Ask if any details need changing

## Quick Commands

| User Says | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| "Make it shorter" | Condense to essential points |
| "More formal" | Use more formal language |
| "More friendly" | Slightly warmer tone (still professional) |
| "Add [detail]" | Incorporate specific information |
